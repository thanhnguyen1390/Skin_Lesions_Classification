Introduction Analysis Models

Automate Skin Lesion Classification Using Deep Neural Network

Skin Lesion Distribution

The dataset has imbalanced classes with more than 60% of the cases are melanocytic nevi while vascular lesions and dermatofibroma are on the lower end with less than 2% of the cases, and other types are also less than melanocytic nevi. Imbalanced classes are not ideal in modeling as they may cause biases in predicting ability of the model toward the major classes and also create problem in preparing samples for train and test sets to include representative across all classes

Training

Stratified sampling is used to prepare train and test sets (80% and 20%) so that every class is present in the sets and follows the sample distribution. Class weights are introduced into the models to make them more sensitive toword Melanoma as well as to reduce the possibility of overfitting of melanocytic nevi. The models are trained using Pytorch pretrained models, AlexNet and ResNet18
